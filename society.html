Rise in Dengue Cases Alarms Kolkata Residents

By: ________ · Date: ________ · Place: Kolkata

Kolkata is witnessing a surge in dengue cases this monsoon, with over 2,500 confirmed in the past month, according to the State Health Department. Hospitals report pressure on bed capacity as cases climb in North Kolkata, Behala, and Salt Lake.

Civic bodies began special drives to clean drains, spray repellents, and run awareness campaigns. Doctors urged early diagnosis and advised eliminating stagnant water to curb Aedes aegypti breeding.

Massive Drop in Student Attendance Raises Concerns in West Bengal Schools

By: ________ · Date: ________ · Place: ________

State surveys show attendance in government schools has fallen from 85% in 2019 to 63% in 2025. Causes include pandemic after-effects, child labour, economic stress, and online distractions.

The government launched a “Back to School” drive with free meals, textbooks, uniforms, and door-to-door outreach. NGOs provide evening tuitions and digital access, yet infrastructure gaps and teacher shortages persist.

Sharp Rise in Dengue Cases Across West Bengal: Data Reveals Concerning Trend

By: ________ · Date: ________ · Place: Kolkata, West Bengal

State Health Department figures indicate 48,732 dengue cases between January and August 2025, a 32% rise over 2024. Kolkata leads with 15,200 cases, followed by North 24 Parganas (8,450), Howrah (6,320), and Hooghly (4,700). Mortality has climbed to 68 recorded deaths.

The surge began with early monsoon rains: January–March 3,210; April–May 5,980; June 9,450; July 14,890; August (till 10th) 15,202. Experts cite stagnant water, poor drainage, and prolonged humidity as key drivers.
